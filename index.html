About

I'm a first year PhD student in the Center of Vision, Speech and Signal Processing at the University of Surrey (UK).
I am fortunate to be supervised by Prof. Adrian Hilton and Dr. Charles Malleson.
My research interests include 3D reconstruction of animals from images and videos,
computer vision and graphics, and machine learning.

Currently my research involves investigating deep learning methods for non-contact
measurement and generation of 3D animal motion and appearance.

A little about me:
I was born in Vietnam, but was brought up in Belgium.
I am trilingual (English, Dutch and French) and currenlty learning Spanish (Stage 1, Stage 2).
In addition to doing research I love real-time technology and animation, reading,
playing music, nature, meeting people and learning new things!
